Advanced Cutting & Welding Technology for Industrial Automation

Technology Overview

Robot Laser Processing represents the cutting edge of modern manufacturing technology, perfectly combining the precise control of industrial robots with the high energy density of laser systems. This comprehensive technology suite encompasses both laser cutting and laser welding, providing complete solutions for complex three-dimensional material processing and joining applications.

Why Choose Robot Laser Processing?

In the context where traditional manufacturing methods cannot meet the demands of modern precision manufacturing, robot laser processing has become the preferred technology for high-end industries such as automotive, aerospace, electronics, and heavy manufacturing, thanks to its non-contact, high-precision, high-speed, and highly flexible characteristics.

Compared to traditional manual operations or conventional automated systems, robot laser processing offers significant advantages including exceptional precision, minimal material waste, small heat-affected zones, superior cut/weld quality, and unmatched speed. These technical advantages are particularly evident when handling complex geometries, thin-wall materials, dissimilar material processing, and intricate three-dimensional operations.

🔥 Laser Welding Technology

High-precision joining technology utilizing focused laser beams to create deep penetration welds with minimal heat input. Perfect for complex joint configurations and dissimilar materials.

✂️ Laser Cutting Technology

Ultra-precise material removal technology capable of intricate 2D and 3D cutting operations. Ideal for complex contours, fine details, and thick material processing.

Key Technical Principles

Laser-Material Interaction

The high-intensity laser beam rapidly heats the material surface. For cutting applications, the beam melts and vaporizes material while assist gas removes molten debris. For welding applications, controlled energy input creates deep penetration keyhole welding with minimal thermal distortion.

Robotic Control Integration

Advanced motion control algorithms synchronize the robot's movement with laser parameters, enabling real-time adjustment of processing speed, power, focus position, and gas flow for optimal results across both cutting and welding operations.

Multi-Axis Processing Capability

6-axis and 7-axis robotic systems enable complex 3D processing paths, allowing for sophisticated cuts, bevels, and weld joints that would be impossible with conventional fixed-head systems.

Core Technical Advantages

Ultra-High Precision Control

Robot repeatability accuracy up to ±0.02mm, combined with micrometer-level laser precision control, achieves extremely high cutting and welding quality consistency. Suitable for precision parts and high-requirement product manufacturing across both processes.

High-Speed Processing Efficiency

Laser cutting speeds up to 15m/min and welding speeds 5-10 times faster than traditional methods. Combined with robot continuous operation capability, dramatically improving production efficiency with 24/7 uninterrupted operation capability.

Minimal Heat Affected Zone

Highly concentrated laser energy results in extremely small heat-affected zones for both cutting and welding, avoiding material property degradation. Particularly suitable for heat-sensitive materials while maintaining excellent original material characteristics.

Superior Surface Quality

Smooth cut edges and aesthetically pleasing weld surfaces requiring minimal post-processing. High-quality finishes meet the demands of high-end products requiring both functionality and aesthetics in cutting and joining operations.

Dual-Process Capability

Single robotic system can perform both cutting and welding operations with quick tool changes. One equipment setup handles complete fabrication workflows, dramatically improving equipment utilization and production flexibility.

Non-Contact Processing

Laser processing requires no physical contact, avoiding tool wear and contamination. Can operate in clean environments, meeting strict requirements of medical, electronics, aerospace, and precision manufacturing industries.

3D Processing Capability

Multi-axis robot systems enable complex 3D cutting paths and weld joints impossible with conventional methods. Process tubes, complex geometries, and intricate assemblies with unprecedented flexibility.

Material Versatility

Process wide range of materials including steel, aluminum, stainless steel, titanium, and exotic alloys. Both cutting and welding operations adaptable to various material thicknesses and compositions.

Cutting & Welding Process Flow

Standard Operating Procedure

1. Workpiece Preparation and Positioning

Workpiece cleaning, clamping, and positioning verification. Ensure joint preparation meets specifications and fixtures provide adequate access for robotic welding.

2. System Parameter Setup

Configure laser power, welding speed, wire feed rate, and shielding gas flow based on material thickness and joint design. Load appropriate welding program.

3. Robot Path Programming

Program welding trajectory using teach pendant or offline programming software. Verify path clearance and optimize motion for cycle time.

4. Vision System Calibration

Calibrate CCD vision system for seam tracking and quality monitoring. Establish inspection parameters and alarm thresholds.

5. Automated Welding Execution

Execute programmed welding sequence with real-time monitoring. System automatically adjusts parameters based on feedback from vision and sensors.

6. Quality Inspection and Assessment

Perform online quality inspection including weld appearance and dimensional measurement. Non-conforming parts are automatically flagged and segregated.

Laser Cutting Process Flow

1. Material Preparation and Setup

Material surface cleaning, fixture installation, and workpiece positioning. Verify material type, thickness, and cutting program compatibility.

2. Cutting Parameter Configuration

Set laser power, cutting speed, assist gas type/pressure, and focus position based on material specifications. Load appropriate cutting program.

3. Tool Path Programming

Generate 3D cutting path using CAM software or teach pendant programming. Optimize cutting sequence and approach angles for efficiency.

4. Height Control Calibration

Calibrate capacitive height control system for standoff distance maintenance. Set collision detection and edge-finding parameters.

5. Automated Cutting Execution

Execute programmed cutting sequence with real-time monitoring. System automatically adjusts focus and gas flow based on material feedback.

6. Quality Control and Finishing

Perform dimensional inspection and edge quality assessment. Automated sorting separates parts meeting specifications from those requiring rework.

Cutting Process Technology

Assist Gas Technology

Sophisticated gas delivery systems provide material-specific assist gases to optimize cutting quality and speed. Automated gas switching enables processing of different materials without manual intervention.

Oxygen Cutting

High-speed cutting of carbon steel with excellent edge quality. Oxygen assists in the oxidation process, enabling faster cutting speeds for thick materials.

Nitrogen Cutting

Inert atmosphere cutting prevents oxidation, providing clean edges for stainless steel and aluminum. Essential for applications requiring oxide-free cuts.

Compressed Air

Cost-effective solution for general purpose cutting of thin materials. Suitable for applications where edge oxidation is acceptable.

Height Control Systems

Capacitive height control maintains precise standoff distance throughout the cutting process. This technology is essential for consistent cut quality when processing warped materials or complex 3D surfaces.

Focus Control Technology

Dynamic focus adjustment optimizes beam characteristics for different material thicknesses and cutting requirements. Automatic focus positioning ensures optimal energy density at the cutting front.
